The same bioreactor hardware and E. coli cell line as Study 001. A culture engineered to express GFP and a target peptide drug was scraped from an agar plate and inoculated into 500 mL of growth medium.

The inoculated medium was loaded into the 450 mL SLA-printed bioreactor using a peristaltic pump. Inlet and outlet ports were sealed with sterile syringe filters (Tisch PP, Part SF14690, Lot #316067213; 25 mm diameter, 0.22 µm pore size, hydrophobic polypropylene membrane).

The bioreactor was oriented horizontally on a rack with the inlet/outlet face toward the operator. Culture fluid pools at the lowest point of the vessel. The peristaltic pump circulates atmospheric air through the culture: filtered air enters through the pump inlet, bubbles through the liquid via the inlet protuberance, and exits through the outlet protuberance through a filter — providing aeration and partial headspace gas exchange. The pump operated on a 10-minute ON / 1-minute OFF duty cycle to allow the motor to cool between cycles.

Sensor configuration evolved across sessions:

Sessions 1–9 (2026-06-15 to 2026-06-16 ~18:30): SHT30 and BME688 #1 on the benchtop adjacent to the bioreactor. The outlet filter was temporarily removed during the first overnight period.

Mid-study: BME688 #2 was repositioned and secured at the bioreactor exhaust outlet, approximately 30 cm (12 in) above BME688 #1 on the rack, to directly sample gas exiting the culture headspace. At this position BME688 #2 measures exhaust temperature (the closest available proxy for headspace temperature) and culture-derived volatile organic compounds.

Later sessions (from 2026-06-16 ~18:34): AS7341 11-channel spectral sensor added as a turbidity proxy for culture optical density.

Note: the peristaltic pump motor stopped responding to Arduino commands at an undetermined point 24–48 hours before data logging ended (root cause unknown). Normal pump operation resumed after restarting the Arduino.
